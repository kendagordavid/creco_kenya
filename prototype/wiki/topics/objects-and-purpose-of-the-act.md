# Objects and Purpose of the PBO Act

The Public Benefit Organizations Act, 2013 was assented on 14 January 2013 and commenced on **14 May 2024**. It replaces the NGO Coordination Act of 1990.

## Main objects (Section 3)

The Act aims to:

1. **Encourage and support PBOs** in meeting the diverse needs of the people of Kenya
2. **Create a conducive environment** for the growth of the PBO sector
3. **Promote self-regulation** among public benefit organizations
4. **Protect freedoms** of expression, association, and peaceful assembly
5. **Facilitate collaboration** between PBOs, government, and other actors
6. **Promote compliance** with legal obligations while safeguarding freedom of association
7. **Facilitate mechanisms** for funding and participation of PBOs in development

## Government responsibilities (Section 4)

The Government has a responsibility to consult and cooperate with PBOs, and to ensure an enabling environment for their operations.

## Why this matters for PBOs

The Act provides a modern framework for registration, operation, and regulation — intended to be more transparent and supportive of civil society than the previous NGO regime.
