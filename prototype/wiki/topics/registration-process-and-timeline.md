# PBO Registration Process and Timeline

## How registration works

1. The organization **submits an application** with all required documents (see [[registration-requirements]])
2. The **PBO Regulatory Authority** receives and reviews the application
3. The Authority **determines** whether to register or reject the application
4. If registered, the organization is entered in the **public registry** of PBOs

## Timeline

Under Section 9(1) of the PBO Act:

> The Authority shall, **within sixty (60) days** after receipt of the application for registration, register the organization as a PBO if satisfied that the application meets the registration requirements.

If the Authority is not satisfied, it may reject the application. Organizations experiencing delays beyond this period may report their experience through CRECO's monitoring channels (production platform feature).

## Public registry

Section 15 requires the Authority to maintain a **public registry of all registered PBOs** accessible via the internet. This supports transparency in the civic space.

## Practical tips for applicants

- Ensure your constitution meets PBO Act requirements before applying
- Include complete founder details and clear public benefit purposes
- Keep copies of everything submitted
- Note the date of submission to track the 60-day window
