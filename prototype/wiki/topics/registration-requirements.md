# PBO Registration Requirements

Section 8 of the PBO Act sets out what an organization must submit when applying for registration as a Public Benefit Organization.

## Required documents

An application for registration shall be accompanied by:

1. **A copy of the constitution** or other constitutive document of the PBO
2. **Names and addresses of the founders** of the PBO
3. **The public benefit purposes** and the **principal activities** the PBO will engage in
4. **The postal and physical address** of the principal place of doing business
5. **The prescribed registration fee**

## Constitution requirements

The Act requires provisions consistent with public benefit status, including:

- The organization's **income and property are not distributable** to any person
- **Members or the governing body have no rights** in the property or assets solely by virtue of membership
- On **dissolution**, remaining assets after liabilities are met shall be transferred to another PBO with similar objectives

## Who must register?

- New organizations seeking PBO status must apply under the PBO Act
- NGOs previously registered under the repealed NGO Coordination Act are **deemed registered** as PBOs under transitional provisions
- Other NGOs had up to **one year from 14 May 2024** (commencement date) to register as a PBO
