# What is a Public Benefit Organization (PBO)?

A **Public Benefit Organization (PBO)** is a voluntary membership grouping of individuals or organizations that is **organized and operated for public benefit purposes** on a **non-profit basis**.

## Key characteristics

- Operates for **public benefit purposes** — serving the public good rather than private profit
- Is **non-profit** — income and property are not distributable to members for private gain
- Is **voluntary** — formed by choice of members or founders
- Must be **registered under the PBO Act** to enjoy the benefits and protections the Act provides

## Kiswahili summary

**Shirika la Faida ya Umma (PBO)** ni kikundi cha hiari cha watu au mashirika kinachofanya kazi kwa **madhumuni ya faida ya umma** na **bila faida ya kibinafsi**.

## Why registration matters

Under Section 6 of the PBO Act, a public benefit organization **shall be registered** under the Act for it to enjoy the benefits that accrue under the Act. Unregistered organizations may not access the legal framework, protections, and benefits intended for PBOs.
